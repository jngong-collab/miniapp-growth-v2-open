# Warm Meridian

This visual direction treats summer care as structured warmth rather than noisy promotion. The composition should feel like a carefully layered clinic artifact: calm gradients, rounded fields, measured spacing, and a central emphasis on ritual continuity. The work must feel meticulously crafted, with every block and line refined until it looks inevitable.

Color should communicate seasonal confidence. Use sun-warmed amber, herbal green, muted coral, and soft ivory as the core palette. These tones should not feel playful or juvenile; they should feel like the result of deep expertise and painstaking calibration, suggesting care, safety, and quiet vitality without slipping into cliché wellness design.

The hierarchy must be instantly legible from a distance. The package name and `899` price anchor the canvas, while the treatment structure is expressed through repeated capsules, badges, and modular count markers. Rhythm matters here: five-and-five-and-five should read as a disciplined system, the product of master-level execution rather than decorative clutter.

Typography should be restrained but intentional. Large Chinese display text should carry authority, while smaller labels act like clinical annotations. Minimal words, strong placement, generous breathing room. The final piece should look labored over with care, as though every alignment and proportion was adjusted by someone at the top of their field.

Illustrative elements should be abstracted from heat, meridians, and treatment cycles: rising arcs, diffused rings, rounded herbal forms, and luminous path lines. Nothing literal, nothing cartoonish. The image must imply a full seasonal program through composition alone, with the polish and cohesion of a premium treatment poster that took countless hours to perfect.
